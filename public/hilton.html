Hilton/BLV Scholarship Program for Disadvantaged Youth

Through a new relationship with Hilton Hanoi Opera and Hilton Garden Inn properties in Hanoi, Vietnam, BLV is expanding its scholarship program for five fortunate students and their families.

In addition to BLV's base scholarship, recipients of the Hilton/BLV Scholarship Program for Disadvantaged Youth will receive these exclusive benefits:

Attend an occupational orientation workship that will prepare them to work in the tourism and hospitality industry and further guidance for future employment opportunities

Prospect for the family's farm products to be bought by Hilton at a preferred price.

Financial support to cover school-related expenses for one school year.

A city tour of Hanoi including a 2-day, 1-night stay at Hilton Hanoi Opera.

Students and Their Families

Phuong Thu

Phuong Thu lives with grandparents, as her parents abandoned her when she was quite young. Her grandfather has lung cancer, and she began to cry as she discussed how much she loved him and how badly she wanted to be able to care for her family in the future. Phuong Thu is interested in working with Better Life Vietnam to study tourism and hospitality, and dreams of one day working in this field and being able to see the world and support her family. She knows that studying is a way to overcome her hardships. Therefore, she stays up until two in the morning pring over books.

Thuong

At just 16, Thuong has faced many struggles and challenges. Thuong lives with her grandparents, as her parents are working in SaiGon. Her eldest sister was forced into dropping out of school at fourteen to support their family, and Thuong is worried that she will face the same fate. She is an excellent student, and participates in the farmwork as weel as spending long hours studying for school. At 5am she wakes up, feeds the chickens, helps with her younger brother, and prepares breakfast.

Vu Thi Thu Trang

One of three children, she lives with her grandfather and disabled father. Her father faces discrimination and abusive employers in the workplace due to his disability. Although he wants to support his children he has faced many struggles. Her family raises chickens, and hopes to work with Better Life Vietnam to create a sustainable work model to raise money for their children's educations. Vu Thi Thu Trang wakes up at four in the morning to cook rice and begin work on the farm. Her grandfather had tears in his eyes as he discussed the value of education to him, and how he knew that education was one of the only ways out of poverty.

Khuat Thi Nhung

Her mother works in a garment shop, where she makes clothers for upwards of ten hours a day. She desperately doesn't want the same life for her daughter, and relizes her daughters potential for success. Her dream for Khuat Thi Nhung is to see her become a teacher and be able to finish her education.

Thao Linh

Her father left her family with a huge gambling debt. He even used the deed to their house to pay off his gambling fees. Most of the during the visit, Thao Link was in the back sewing garments for a factory. She only took a break to briefly have her picture taken, citing that the only way she could stay in school was if she continued to work throughout the day. They sell 100 of these pairs of pants for 30,000 dong (approx. $1.50). Thao Linh wants to be a fashion designer when she grows up. However, most of all she just wants to be able to finish her education and support her family.

Hilton Hanoi Opera and Hilton Garden Inn Hanoi Partner with Better Life Vietnam

to Extend Opportunities to Young People

HANOI, Vietnam – September 18, 2017 – Better Life Vietnam (BLV), together with Hilton Hanoi Opera and Hilton Garden Inn Hanoi, today announced their collaboration to offer more learning and development opportunities for young people in Vietnam. In this partnership, both Hilton hotels in Hanoi will provide funding support and hospitality careers workshop for five recipients of the Disadvantaged Youth Scholarship run by BLV, a non-profit that works to improve education conditions for underprivileged children and youth in Vietnam. “Our partnership with Better Life Vietnam bears testament to Hilton’s commitment to connect, prepare and employ at least one million young people by 2019,” said Andrew Nisbet, Cluster General Manger for Hilton Hanoi Opera and Hilton Garden Inn Hanoi. “Guided by our mission to be the most hospitable company in the world, we remain dedicated to work with local community organizations and participate in the progress and development of our local community.” “At BLV, we want the whole family involved and committed to working for a brighter future,” says Thinh Nguyen, Founder of Better Life Vietnam. “It’s more than just receiving financial support for school expenses. We conduct interviews, review student school records and require the family to propose an income-generating model such as raising chickens or growing organic vegetables to ensure they are set up for a sustainable future before selecting our recipients. It’s a spiritual gift filled with hope and belief in a better life for our students and their families.” Recipients of the Disadvantaged Youth Scholarship have been selected based on their excellent academic performance in school. In addition to getting support for their continued education in school, the students will also have a chance to learn more about careers in the hospitality during the year. Through on-site visits, hand-on experience and chances to connect with tourism and hospitality professionals at the Hilton hotels in Hanoi, the students will gain better insights and access to developing a career in the hospitality industry. Furthermore, families whose child is part of the scholarship would also be positively impacted. Families that run a farming business stand a chance to have their agricultural produce reviewed and considered as a source of food supply for both hotels.

About Better Life Vietnam

Founded in 2010, Better Life Vietnam (BLV) is a non-profit organization that works to improve education conditions for underprivileged children and youth in Vietnam. In 2016, Better Life Vietnam was registered as a Social Enterprise, under the name, Cuong Thinh BLV Social Co., LTD. With the vision to help reduce the burden for the poor families of Vietnam, Better Life Vietnam has dedicated its efforts to encourage knowledge and support children in getting the education they deserve. The organization’s three main projects include Books Library, Scholarship, and English Class. For more information, visit betterlifevietnam.org or find us on Facebook, Twitter and Instagram. In order to sustain BLV, Cuong Thinh BLV Social Co., LTD launched Tours For Books, a social enterprise that organizes tours and travel services in Vietnam to generate funds to support the education projects at Better Life Vietnam. For more information, visit vietnamtoursforbooks.com. On the web: www.betterlifevietnam.org BLV Facebook: https://www.facebook.com/BetterLifeVN BLV Twitter: https://twitter.com/betterlifevn

About Hilton Hanoi Opera

Strategically located in the heart of Hanoi’s central business district, Hilton Hanoi Opera is within easy walking distance of the city’s tourist attractions and bustling shopping areas, providing an ideal home from which to experience and explore the rich textures of Hanoi. Inspired by the character of the famous Opera House opposite, the hotel is a fusion of traditions: gentle Vietnamese hospitality and graciousness, and Hilton’s assurance of total reliability, impeccable service and first-class facilities.
